good side

I’ll share the reasons a good friend of mine working as a Director of Software Development prefers the ‘ghetto’ over a ‘good neighborhood.’ Let me start off by saying that the ‘ghetto’ where he lives is called ghetto because it’s a neighborhood in transition, meaning it changes from block to block. People like him who choose to live there don’t refer to it as ghetto, but rather as ‘edgy,’ ‘urban,’ and ‘hip.’ My friend earns so much money, he could live just about anywhere he wanted to, including Palo Alto, Mark Zuckerberg’s Mission neighborhood, the Marina, Pac Heights, you name it. But he loves living in his current neighborhood because he feels he’s actually paying a fair price for the house he bought, as opposed to paying a stratospheric mortgage elsewhere. He likes the fact that he can bike to BART, no car needed, that he can dress casual and no one thinks or suspects he has money, and that there are few, if any chain stores or restaurants down the street from where he lives. His beat up old car remains safe parked on the same spot without anyone attempting to break in. The only reason why some consider his neighborhood ghetto is that it looks unkempt and there is some level of dereliction. But he insists all of that is harmless and keeps the neighborhood ‘real.’ As he is a single guy with no dependents and with nothing expensive to guard, he’s not concerned about how pretty the neighborhood looks.
